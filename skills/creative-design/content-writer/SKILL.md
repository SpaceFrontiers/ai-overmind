# Content Writer

Create compelling content that engages your audience and achieves your goals.

## Instructions

When creating content:

1. **Understand the Brief**: Clarify purpose, audience, and goals
2. **Research the Topic**: Gather information and insights
3. **Define the Angle**: Choose unique perspective or approach
4. **Structure Content**: Organize for maximum impact
5. **Write Engaging Copy**: Hook, inform, and inspire action
6. **Edit Ruthlessly**: Cut fluff, strengthen weak points
7. **Optimize**: Headlines, formatting, SEO (if applicable)

## Content Types

### Blog Post
- 800-2000 words typically
- SEO-optimized title and headers
- Clear structure with subheadings
- Introduction, body, conclusion
- Call to action at end

### Social Media Post
- Platform-specific length (Twitter: 280 chars, LinkedIn: longer form)
- Hook in first line
- Value or entertainment focus
- Hashtags (2-5 relevant ones)
- Call to action or question for engagement

### Marketing Copy
- Benefit-focused (not feature-focused)
- Clear value proposition
- Emotional connection
- Strong CTA
- Scannable format

### Technical Article
- Clear, jargon-free explanations
- Step-by-step when applicable
- Code examples or visuals
- Practical applications
- Resources and next steps

## Writing Framework: AIDA

### Attention
Grab attention with compelling hook:
- Surprising statistic
- Provocative question
- Relatable problem
- Bold statement

### Interest
Build interest by:
- Explaining the problem
- Sharing insights
- Telling a story
- Providing context

### Desire
Create desire through:
- Showing benefits
- Painting the picture
- Sharing proof (data, testimonials)
- Addressing objections

### Action
Drive action with:
- Clear CTA
- Specific next steps
- Urgency or incentive
- Easy to execute

## Style Guidelines

### Voice and Tone

**Professional**
- Formal language
- Third person
- Industry terminology
- Data-driven

**Conversational**
- Casual language
- First/second person
- Simple terms
- Story-driven

**Authoritative**
- Expert positioning
- Confident statements
- Research-backed
- Detailed explanations

**Friendly**
- Warm and approachable
- Personal anecdotes
- Humor when appropriate
- Encouraging

### Writing Rules

**Clarity**
- Short sentences (15-20 words average)
- Active voice over passive
- Simple words over complex
- One idea per sentence

**Engagement**
- Vary sentence length
- Use metaphors and analogies
- Include examples
- Ask questions
- Tell stories

**Scannability**
- Subheadings every 200-300 words
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Bullet points and lists
- Bold key points
- White space

## Example: Blog Post Structure

```markdown
# How to [Achieve Desired Outcome]: [Number] [Proven/Simple/Effective] Strategies

## Introduction (100-150 words)
Hook: [Relatable problem or surprising fact]
Context: [Why this matters]
Promise: [What reader will learn]

## Section 1: [First Strategy]
Explanation: [What it is]
Why it works: [The reasoning]
How to do it:
  1. Step one
  2. Step two
  3. Step three
Example: [Real-world application]

## Section 2: [Second Strategy]
[Same structure]

## Section 3: [Third Strategy]
[Same structure]

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Mistake 1: [What not to do and why]
- Mistake 2: [What not to do and why]

## Conclusion
Summary: [Recap key points]
CTA: [What to do next]
```

## SEO Writing Guidelines

### Keywords
- Natural integration
- In title, first paragraph, headers
- Don't keyword stuff
- Use variations and synonyms

### Structure
- H1 (one per page): Main keyword
- H2s: Topic keywords
- H3s: Subtopics
- Meta description: 150-160 chars

### Internal Linking
- Link to related content
- Descriptive anchor text
- Help readers navigate
- Boost SEO

## Headline Formulas

- **How-To**: "How to [Achieve Result] in [Timeframe]"
- **List**: "[Number] Ways to [Achieve Result]"
- **Question**: "Are You [Making Common Mistake]?"
- **Negative**: "Stop [Doing Something Wrong]"
- **Secret**: "The [Adjective] Secret to [Desired Outcome]"
- **Reason Why**: "Why [Unexpected Fact]"

## Common Mistakes

- **Burying the Lead**: Get to the point quickly
- **Walls of Text**: Break into paragraphs and lists
- **Passive Voice**: "Mistakes were made" → "We made mistakes"
- **Jargon Overload**: Write for your audience, not experts
- **No CTA**: Always tell reader what to do next
- **Generic Headlines**: Be specific and benefit-focused
- **Ignoring Audience**: Write for reader, not yourself
- **No Editing**: First draft is never final

## Research-Backed Persuasion Science

### The Psychology of Persuasive Communication
Research on persuasion techniques (Braca & Dondio, 2023) reveals systematic approaches to influencing behavior:

**Key Persuasion Principles**:
- **Reciprocity**: People feel obligated to return favors
- **Scarcity**: Limited availability increases perceived value
- **Authority**: Expertise and credentials build trust
- **Consistency**: People align with previous commitments
- **Liking**: We're influenced by those we like
- **Social Proof**: Others' behavior guides our decisions

**Application to Content**:
- Use testimonials and user statistics (social proof)
- Highlight credentials and expertise (authority)
- Create urgency with limited-time offers (scarcity)
- Reference reader's past interests (consistency)

### Copywriting Effectiveness
Research on marketing communication (Bun et al., 2025) demonstrates:
- **Copywriting positively affects purchase intention** through clear, persuasive messaging
- **Content marketing builds brand awareness** which mediates buying behavior
- **Combined approach**: Copywriting + content marketing + brand awareness = strongest effect
- **Emotional connection**: Messages that resonate emotionally outperform purely rational appeals

**Practical Takeaway**: Don't just list features. Tell stories that create emotional connections while building your authority.

### Professional Writing Best Practices
Communication research (Long, 2020) emphasizes six core principles:

1. **Clear call to action**: Define the physical response you want before writing anything
2. **Clarity over cleverness**: Straightforward messaging beats wordplay
3. **Avoid fact overload**: People decide with hearts first, then backfill with logic
4. **Accessibility**: Conversational tone with short paragraphs (4-5 lines max)
5. **Eliminate weak words**: Remove "very" and overused adverbs
6. **Strong endings**: Final words are most remembered - restate your CTA

**Evidence-Based Insight**: Studies show readers rarely make decisions based solely on facts. Lead with emotional resonance, support with evidence.
